# Identity Interview Guide

A structured 3-round interview to create your `me.md` identity file. This is the most important file in your vault -- it tells every AI tool who you are, how you think, and how to work with you.

You can run this interview two ways:
1. **With Claude Code** -- Use the `/ideaverse-setup` skill. It asks the questions interactively and compiles your answers into me.md automatically.
2. **Self-administered** -- Use this guide. Answer each question below, then copy your answers into the me.md skeleton at `_ai-OS/me.md`.

---

## Round 1: Who You Are

### Q1: Summary Statement

> In 2-3 sentences, describe what drives you. What are you building toward? What motivates you to keep going?

Write this as a personal manifesto, not a resume objective. Think about what you believe, not just what you do.

**Goes into:** me.md > Me > The Summary Statement

---

### Q2: First Principles

> What are the 3-5 beliefs you don't compromise on? These are the load-bearing walls of your worldview.

Format each as: **On [Topic]:** [explanation of the belief and why it matters to you]

Examples of topics: problems, learning, efficiency, growth, success, integrity, craft.

**Goes into:** me.md > Me > First Principles

---

### Q3: Values

> List your top 5-6 values in priority order. When two values conflict, the higher-ranked one wins.

For each value, add a one-sentence explanation of what it means to you specifically.

**Goes into:** me.md > Me > Values

---

## Round 2: How You Think

### Q4: Thinking Style

> How do you naturally approach problems? Are you a pattern matcher, first-principles thinker, builder, researcher, or something else?

Give 3-5 bullet points describing your cognitive style. Be honest about both strengths and gaps.

**Goes into:** me.md > How I Think > Thinking Style

---

### Q5: Intellectual Influences

> Who has shaped how you think? Could be authors, YouTube creators, coworkers, mentors. For each person, identify what they specifically taught you.

Don't just name-drop. The value is in identifying *what* each influence taught you, not just who they are.

**Goes into:** me.md > How I Think > Intellectual Influences

---

### Q6: Spark List

> What topics and project ideas are buzzing in your head right now? What would you build if time were unlimited?

This captures your current energy. It changes over time -- that's fine. Review it monthly.

**Goes into:** Not directly in me.md. Keep this as a separate note or append to the Summary Statement.

---

## Round 3: Work Preferences

### Q7: Inner Spaces

> Describe your productive environments as metaphorical "spaces." Do you have a workshop mode? An office mode? When and where does your best work happen?

Think about the different mental states you operate in and what triggers each one.

**Goes into:** me.md > How I Think > Inner Spaces

---

### Q8: Vibe

> How should an AI assistant talk to you? What communication patterns annoy you? What do you want more of?

Be specific. Examples: "Skip the praise, just answer." "Use tables over paragraphs." "Be opinionated, not neutral." "Don't end with questions."

**Goes into:** me.md > How to Work With Me > Vibe + Preferences

---

## After the Interview

1. Open `_ai-OS/me.md` in your vault
2. Fill in each section with your answers
3. Add any rules or conventions under the Rules section (communication style, process preferences, domain-specific conventions)
4. Update the Tool Pointers section with your actual vault path
5. Set a calendar reminder to review me.md monthly

The file is most valuable when it's honest and specific. Generic answers produce generic AI behavior. The sharper your answers, the better every AI tool will work with you.
